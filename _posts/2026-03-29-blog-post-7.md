## Mar 30th - Apr 19th (Near-Term Plan and Milestone)
We will perform a final inspection of the machined parts to ensure all tolerances meet specifications before proceeding with assembly and installation. This will be followed by a bench mounting and alignment verification on the mock up station. The team will continue to collaborate on the control programming shared on GITHUB along doing wirings and properly intergrating electrical component inside the enclosure, ensuring proper cable management for organization and protection. Finally, we will verify safety features are working and test the combined motion sequences in slow speed. 

## Next two weeks (Obstacles + Solutions + Backup plans)
We might encounter overheating risk associate with having 3 power supply and potential heat damage to the microcontrollers. Our team will monitor ambient temperature within the enclosure during test run if we experience overheating pasted operating temperature, we will take steps to migitate by implimenting active cooling system such as fans. The second risk is cable getting tangle or tugged in between steps, to counter this we will ensure proper cable managment. 

Over the next two weeks as we work to build our control logic we may encounter obstacles such as incorrect motor parameters, communication errors, or firmware conflicts. These can be resolved by verifying wiring and configurations, using manufacturer setup tools, and performing incremental testing; backup plans include reloading configurations, testing with known working components, or consulting technical support. Safety verification may reveal issues such as improper emergency stop function. Testing safety circuits under load, installing guards, and confirming proper fuse ratings will reduce hazards, while redundant safety devices and software motion limits can provide additional protection. Finally, when testing combined motion sequences, potential collisions, synchronization errors, or vibration may occur. Running low-speed tests, tuning motion parameters, and monitoring structural behavior will help ensure smooth operation, with limit switches, sequence adjustments, or structural reinforcement available if further refinement is required.
