Hi again, as the end of the semester approaches, the Coogineers have been pushing hard toward bringing the whole system together ranging from last minutes machining to reduce load, to troubleshooting programs and refining program sequencing. Here, we present to you the current status of our AMOFT. 

## 1. Progress made from the period of March 30 to April 19: 

a) Completed full mechanical system assembly, including winch cable installation.  
b) Designed, prototyped, and 3D printed a remote control box with RUN, RESET, and STOP functions, integrated with status indicator lights.  
c) Programmed and verified a functional emergency stop (E-stop) system.  
d) Installed two limit switches to define and control MOFT positioning.  
e) Organized and labeled all electrical wiring.  
f) Prototyped mounting brackets for limit switches.  

## 2. Validation results + Discussions

At this stage, key safety and control functions have been successfully validated, including the E-stop system and limit switches. These results demonstrate that the system can safely interrupt operation and accurately detect predefined positions.

## 3. Improvements + Remaining validation + Next Steps

With the remaining time before the final report, the team will focus on:

a) Validating positional accuracy using proximity sensors.  
b) Assessing repeatability of motion.  
c) Measuring and verifying total cycle time compare.  
d) Documenting validation trials and comparing result for further improvements.
